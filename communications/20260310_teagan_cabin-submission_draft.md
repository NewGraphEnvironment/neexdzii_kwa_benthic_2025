Hope things are going well. Quick update on the benthic work Tieasha and I did on the Neexdzii Kwa last fall.

We collected CABIN kick-net samples at three mainstem sites and have results back from the lab. One of our sites (BUL-01, next to the rest stop just past the North Road bridge outside Houston near the Morice FSR turnoff) is at the same location as CABIN site MOR37 ("Upper Bulkley @ Morice") which was sampled in 2004 and again in 2018 under the Wet'suwet'en ESI study. Adding our 2025 data as a third visit to MOR37 would build a 20-year monitoring record at that site — pretty rare for a CABIN site in the region.

We have our own approved CABIN study ("BC-Neexdzi Kwa") and can upload the data right away. But if you'd prefer the data loaded under the ESI study instead, that would keep the Wet'suwet'en aquatic monitoring record together in one project. Having everything in one place means your team can run BEAST assessments across all your sites and years in a single view, and it strengthens the case for continued monitoring funding down the road.
